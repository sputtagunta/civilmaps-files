## 1. Delaware Court of Chancery § 220 Books and Records Action

**Case Number:** 2024-1296-SEM
**Court:** Delaware Court of Chancery
**Filing Date:** October 2024
**Status:** Active litigation

### What Was Filed

**Verified Complaint** seeking inspection of Solfice Research, Inc. (d/b/a Civil Maps) books and records under Delaware General Corporation Law § 220.

### Purpose of Action

To investigate:
- Whether the June 15, 2022 stockholder vote was properly conducted
- Whether material information was disclosed to stockholders before the vote
- Whether the transaction achieved valid disinterested majority approval
- Whether management (Stefan Safko and Scott Harvey) received undisclosed personal compensation
- Whether the $10.6M sale price was fraudulently suppressed from fair value ($200M)

### Supporting Documents Filed

**In Delaware Chancery Court:**
1. **Verified Complaint** - Initial § 220 demand and basis for inspection
2. **Affidavit of Service** - With Exhibits A-O documenting evidence
3. **Anuj Affidavit** - Stockholder status verification
4. **Sravan Affidavit (Multiple)** - Factual support for plaintiff's claims
5. **Plaintiff Opposition Briefs** - Opposition to defendant's motion to dismiss
6. **Meet and Confer Letters** - Joint status reports and correspondence

**Defendant (Solfice/Luminar) Filed:**
- Motion to Dismiss
- Reply Brief
- Supporting affidavits

### Key Allegations in Complaint

1. **Vote Manipulation:** Management coerced stockholders to vote YES by threatening to reduce purchase price if votes weren't received
2. **Undisclosed Compensation:** Stefan Safko (0% equity) and Scott Harvey (4% equity) received substantial personal employment compensation from Luminar that was never disclosed to stockholders
3. **Fraudulent Valuation:** Company sold for $10.6M despite being worth $200M+ (89% value destruction)
4. **3x Liquidation Preference Collusion:** Management agreed to give preferred shareholders 3x liquidation preference on $2.5M convertible notes to facilitate low-valuation sale
5. **Information Suppression:** Stockholders were not told that Luminar initially indicated willingness to pay $20M+ for assets

## 5. Legal Theories Asserted

### Against Solfice/Civil Maps Management

1. **Breach of Fiduciary Duty of Disclosure**
   - Failed to disclose management's personal compensation
   - Failed to disclose vote coercion by buyer
   - Failed to disclose initial $20M+ price indication

2. **Breach of Fiduciary Duty of Loyalty**
   - Prioritized personal compensation over stockholder value
   - Agreed to 3x liquidation preference to benefit themselves
   - Self-dealing in connection with employment arrangements

3. **Breach of Fiduciary Duty of Care**
   - Sold company for $10.6M when worth $200M+
   - Failed to properly value assets
   - Did not run competitive process

### Against Luminar Technologies

1. **Aiding and Abetting Fiduciary Breaches**
   - Knowing participation in vote manipulation
   - Structured transaction to punish non-consenting stockholders
   - Refused to provide information while demanding proxy votes

2. **Securities Fraud (Federal)**
   - Material omissions in SEC filings
   - Failure to disclose management compensation
   - Failure to disclose disputed ownership of acquired assets
   - Ongoing violations regarding current asset sale considerations

3. **Fraudulent Transfer**
   - Assets transferred for less than fair value
   - Company insolvent or rendered insolvent by transaction
   - Transferee (Luminar) had knowledge of fraud

4. **Constructive Trust**
   - Equitable remedy sought over disputed Civil Maps IP
   - Assets wrongfully obtained through fraud
   - Unjust enrichment of Luminar at stockholders' expense
